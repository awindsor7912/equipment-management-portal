<section class="section" id="overview">
  <div class="container">
    <div class="section-header">
      <h2>Project Overview</h2>
      <p>
        The Equipment Management Portal was developed as a concept for improving how equipment data is
        tracked, organized, and reviewed across operational workflows. The objective was to move away
        from inconsistent manual tracking methods and toward a more reliable, centralized data structure.
      </p>
    </div>

    <div class="card-grid">
      <article class="card">
        <div class="card-meta">
          <span class="tag">Business Problem</span>
        </div>
        <h3>Fragmented Equipment Tracking</h3>
        <p>
          Equipment records are often maintained across disconnected spreadsheets, local files, or
          team-specific tracking methods. This creates inconsistencies in data quality, weakens visibility,
          and makes lifecycle oversight more difficult.
        </p>
        <ul>
          <li>Data ownership can become unclear across teams or regions.</li>
          <li>Manual updates increase the risk of stale or conflicting records.</li>
          <li>Reporting and verification become more time-intensive than necessary.</li>
        </ul>
      </article>

      <article class="card">
        <div class="card-meta">
          <span class="tag">Solution Direction</span>
        </div>
        <h3>Centralized Portal Model</h3>
        <p>
          This concept proposes a centralized portal structure to support cleaner equipment records,
          more consistent lifecycle tracking, and better operational visibility across stakeholders.
        </p>
        <ul>
          <li>Creates a single reference point for equipment-related records.</li>
          <li>Supports more standardized tracking practices.</li>
          <li>Improves the foundation for future reporting and automation.</li>
        </ul>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="section" id="approach">
  <div class="container">
    <div class="section-header">
      <h2>Approach</h2>
      <p>
        The portal concept was structured around data consistency, maintainability, and long-term
        scalability rather than short-term patchwork fixes.
      </p>
    </div>

    <div class="card-grid">
      <article class="card">
        <div class="card-meta">
          <span class="tag">Data Design</span>
          <span class="tag">Relational Thinking</span>
        </div>
        <h3>Structured Data Architecture</h3>
        <p>
          A major design goal was to support more structured equipment data management through
          cleaner data relationships and reduced spreadsheet dependency.
        </p>
        <ul>
          <li>Encourages a more standardized approach to equipment records.</li>
          <li>Supports future transition into relational or Dataverse-backed structures.</li>
          <li>Improves the usability of equipment data for review, validation, and reporting.</li>
        </ul>
      </article>

      <article class="card">
        <div class="card-meta">
          <span class="tag">Operational Visibility</span>
          <span class="tag">Process Improvement</span>
        </div>
        <h3>Better Lifecycle Oversight</h3>
        <p>
          The portal was intended to improve visibility across the equipment lifecycle by making
          status, ownership, and record history easier to manage and review.
        </p>
        <ul>
          <li>Supports clearer tracking of equipment movement and status changes.</li>
          <li>Reduces ambiguity caused by scattered or duplicated records.</li>
          <li>Creates a stronger operational foundation for future controls and reporting.</li>
        </ul>
      </article>

      <article class="card">
        <div class="card-meta">
          <span class="tag">Scalability</span>
          <span class="tag">Future State</span>
        </div>
        <h3>Built for Expansion</h3>
        <p>
          Rather than functioning as a one-off tracker, the concept was framed as a scalable portal
          that could evolve into a broader equipment data management solution.
        </p>
        <ul>
          <li>Supports future integration with enterprise data sources.</li>
          <li>Provides a stronger base for cross-functional use.</li>
          <li>Aligns with a more mature system design approach than isolated tracking files.</li>
        </ul>
      </article>

      <article class="card">
        <div class="card-meta">
          <span class="tag">Documentation</span>
          <span class="tag">Portfolio Use</span>
        </div>
        <h3>Repository Purpose</h3>
        <p>
          This repository serves as a professional documentation space for the portal concept,
          including design intent, structural thinking, and implementation direction.
        </p>
        <ul>
          <li>Captures the business rationale behind the solution.</li>
          <li>Documents the transition from fragmented tracking toward centralized structure.</li>
          <li>Demonstrates systems thinking applied to operational data problems.</li>
        </ul>
      </article>
    </div>

    <div class="band">
      <h3>Project positioning</h3>
      <p>
        This project is best presented as a systems design and operational data management initiative.
        The value is not in decorative interface language. The value is in centralization, cleaner
        tracking logic, better visibility, and a stronger path toward structured equipment data control.
      </p>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>
